## 2. What went well

- **🎯 The headline (and a correction): tests first executed HERE, in Epic 3 — not Epic 4.** The Epic 4 retro credited Epic 4 with closing the two-epic-old test-execution gap (Epic 1 **A2** / Epic 2 **#1**). Epic 4 *scaled* it (120 → 270); Epic 3 *started* it. Skipping this retro is exactly why that credit drifted.
- **The 3-layer review caught Gate-D / English-leak BLOCKERS, not nitpicks.** 3.1's **Patch P3** was the big one — Registreer & Wagwoord-herstel were link-buttons bouncing to **English WP-core screens**; the fix rebuilt them as in-theme Afrikaans forms, closing the AC-1 leakage gap. 3.5 stripped hidden English `[NEEDS HUMAN AFRIKAANS]` spans and swapped a hardcoded `/privaatheidsbeleid` for dynamic `get_privacy_policy_url()`.

R6 defenses are **vetted-plugin seams behind hooks**, never `ink-core` anti-spam logic — three-layer separation intact.
- **The Epic 2 capability debt was finally paid down where roles got defined (3.3).** CPT `capability_type` and staff-only taxonomy term-caps — deferred from 2.1/2.2 — landed in 3.3 via the `Kernel\Capabilities` + `Activation` grant seam, on schedule per Epic 2 action #5.

## 3. What didn't — recurring patterns

1. **The `is_scalar()` shared-helper action (Epic 2 #2) didn't just stall — it regressed.** There is **still no shared sanitize helper**; the guard is re-implemented inline at **11+ sites**, and **each new Epic 3 story added more** (3.3 `Onboarding`, 3.5/3.6 handlers, `Approval`/`Onboarding` per-class `sanitizeFlag()` methods that duplicate the same `is_scalar → default` shape). The idiom is now *well-understood* — which is precisely why it should be extracted once instead of rediscovered per handler. Two epics open.
2. **`skryfwerk` is still absent from `afrikaans-terms.md` (Epic 2 #3).** Confirmed by grep — the migration catch-all CPT remains a source-of-truth drift between glossary and registry. Trivial to close; just never closed.
3. **Afrikaans copy debt accrued here, with no guard at the time.** 3.5 (social divider/consent) and 3.6 (decision-email subjects/bodies) shipped `[NEEDS HUMAN AFRIKAANS]` / `[WAG OP MENSLIKE KOPIE]` placeholders toggle-OFF. At the time there was no automated leak detection. *(Now substantially addressed — see §4.)*
4. **Review carried too much of the load.** 3.5 took **7** post-commit patches and 3.6 took **6**; in 3.5 the dev cycle left tests for the reviewer to execute. The convergent review is working as a safety net, but the volume of leak/correctness fixes caught *after* commit signals dev-side under-execution of the Gate-D and TDD checks that should run before review.

Smaller signals:
- **Docblock truth-drift:** 3.6 shipped a docblock falsely claiming un-reject UI reversibility; caught and corrected in review.
- **Constant-collision near-miss:** 3.6 needed de-aliasing (`_email`/`_login` suffixes) to avoid `META_*` key collisions — a naming-discipline reminder for the registrar pattern.

## 9. Key takeaways

1. **Skipping a retro misattributes wins, not just misses.** Epic 3 is where tests first ran — a flagship process win — and the credit drifted to Epic 4 because Epic 3 had no retro. Banking it now corrects the record and is the strongest argument for keeping the close-step standing.
2. **A well-understood idiom is a candidate for extraction, not an excuse to skip it.** The `is_scalar()` guard is so well-understood that every story re-types it — 11+ sites and growing. Two epics of "it's basically a pattern" is the signal to either extract it or formally close it; not to let it drift a third time.
3. **Defense-in-depth landed without sacrificing frictionless onboarding.** Spike-first (3.4) + everything fail-safe-OFF (3.5/3.6) + hooks-only plugin seams delivered the R6 layer while protecting UJ-1 — a clean template for any future "add a gate without adding friction" work.
