# Story 3.3: Registration lifecycle / onboarding

Status: done

<!-- Note: Validation is optional. Run validate-create-story for quality check before dev-story. -->

## Story

As a new lid,
I want to complete my profile and be gently prompted to take a first social action,
so that I am onboarded without being blocked — entirely in Afrikaans. (FR-2, FR-3)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. **Post-signup onboarding runs as a soft, Afrikaans, skippable flow that never blocks account usage (graceful degradation is the core requirement).** Given a completed signup (the account already exists at **Brons** / **gratis lid** from Story 3.1 — this story picks up *after* account creation), when onboarding runs, then the lid is offered a lightweight **profile-completion** step and **one first social-action prompt** — *volg 'n skrywer* **OR** *stoor 'n bydrae na jou leeslys*. Every step is **skippable** (an "Slaan oor" / "Klaar" affordance), completing or skipping it **does not block** any account capability (read/react/respond/follow/leeslys, per the gratis-lid rights), and the whole flow is one-time (it does not re-nag a returning lid who already finished or dismissed it). _[Source: epics.md#Story-3.3 AC "the lid creates an account → completes profile (gratis lid) → is prompted to follow a skrywer or save a bydrae to leeslys" + "the prompt is skippable, does not block completion, and degrades gracefully with a thin catalogue"; EXPERIENCE.md line 200 ("Prompted to a soft, skippable first social action (follow a skrywer / save a bydrae to her leeslys)"), line 130 ("Post-signup first-action prompt degrades gracefully on a thin catalogue and stays skippable"); architecture.md line 1081 ("3 Accounts & auth | … · ink-core (first-action prompt)"); project-context.md Afrikaans-first / Gate D]_

2. **Every onboarding surface, label, prompt, toast, and any onboarding email is Afrikaans with zero English leakage — sentence case, "jy"-voice, glossary-sourced; no invented or AI-translated copy.** Given the onboarding screen(s) and any onboarding-stage strings, when they render, then all text comes from the **terminology registry** (`Ink\I18n\Terms` / `ink_term()` / the `ink/term` Block Bindings source / `ink_foundation_term()` theme bridge — Story 2.0) and the approved `ui-copy-translations.md` / `afrikaans-terms.md` entries (e.g. **gratis lid**, **leeslys**, **Volg** / **Volg tans**, **bydrae**, **skrywer**, **profiel**, **My Profiel**), addressing the member as **"jy"**, in **sentence case**, with **no English string** reaching the front end. Where the exact onboarding microcopy (welcome line, profile-completion prompt, "Slaan oor" wording) is **not yet authored** in `ui-copy-translations.md`, it is **flagged for human authoring** — never machine-/AI-translated, never lifted from a Lovable mock. _[Source: project-context.md "Front end + user-facing transactional emails are entirely Afrikaans", "No AI-generated Afrikaans", "Controlled-vocabulary UI labels come from the ink-core terminology registry", "Heading casing: sentence case", "Never lift copy from the Lovable mockup"; afrikaans-terms.md lines 46 (gratis lid), 137 (leeslys), 138–140 (volg/volgeling/Volg), 173–174 (Volg / Volg nie meer nie); ui-copy-translations.md lines 155–160 (leeslys/volg toasts), 355 (Skep jou rekening); 2-0-terminology-label-registry.md]_

3. **The first-action prompt PROMPTS toward follow + leeslys but does NOT build those subsystems — it degrades gracefully when they (or a catalogue) are absent.** Given that the **follow graph** is a custom `ink-core` table owned by **Story 9.2 (Epic 9 / `Ink\Social`)** and the **leeslys** is the custom table `ink_reading_list` owned by **Story 7.7 (Epic 7 / `Ink\Engagement`)** — neither of which exists yet — when onboarding shows the first-action prompt, then it presents the *invitation* (a thin catalogue of a few skrywers / bydraes to follow or save) and **degrades gracefully**: if the catalogue is thin or empty, or the follow/leeslys write capability is not yet present, the prompt shows the designed **empty/soft state** ("Jy volg nog niemand nie" / equivalent) and remains **skippable**, and onboarding **never builds a custom table, REST write endpoint (`/ink/v1/volg`, `/ink/v1/leeslys`), or follow/leeslys business logic**. ### What this story is (and is NOT)

All of it **extends the existing `Ink\Accounts` module**. _[epics.md#Story-3.3; architecture.md line 1081 (first-action prompt), AD-6 (editorial caps at activation); epic-2-retro §5; project-context.md]_
- **IS NOT:** the **follow graph** (Story 9.2 / `Ink\Social`) or the **leeslys** store (`ink_reading_list`, Story 7.7 / `Ink\Engagement`) — those subsystems are owned by later epics; this story only prompts toward them and degrades. NOT the reader/writer **intent** gate (Story 3.2 — REMOVED; no `ink_writer_intent`). NOT R6 — **anti-spam** (3.4), **social-login** (3.5), the **"wag op goedkeuring" approval queue** (3.6). NOT the **Lidmaatskap** purchase/PayFast flow (Epic 4) and **no `Ink\Entitlement`** coupling (gratis lid stays gratis lid). - **The follow graph (custom asymmetric table + `/ink/v1/volg`) is Story 9.2; the leeslys (`ink_reading_list` + `/ink/v1/leeslys`) is Story 7.7.** They do not exist yet.
- Therefore the prompt **must degrade gracefully**: present the invitation over a thin catalogue; if the catalogue is thin/empty or the follow/leeslys *write* isn't available, render the designed soft/empty state and stay skippable. The prompt is a **forward seam** — when Epics 7/9 land, it links to / triggers their `Volg` / `Stoor na leeslys` action; until then it no-ops gracefully and never errors. **Do not pre-build Epic 7/9 subsystems** (no custom table, no REST write, no follow/leeslys business logic) merely to make the prompt "work" — graceful degradation IS the working behaviour at this point in the build order.

### ⭐ The role/capability mapping (the deferred Epic-2 work — genuinely in 3.3 scope)

This is **explicitly in 3.3 scope** per **epics.md#Story-3.3** ("Deferred from Epic 2 review … when member/editor roles are defined here, set CPT `capability_type`/`capabilities` … and restrict taxonomy term-management caps") and confirmed by **epic-2-retro §5 + action #5**. Handle it as a **tightly-scoped sub-deliverable**, not an editorial-role redesign:

- **Roles:** the gratis lid is the WordPress **`subscriber`** (the default for a self-registered account — no custom member role is required just to satisfy this story; engagement is gated on `is_user_logged_in()` + nonce, not a capability, per AD-6). The **`editor` role (redakteur)** receives the four Kernel custom caps (`ink_manage_tiers`/`_challenges`/`_sponsors`/`ink_moderate`) **at activation** (AD-6 line 463–465). Wire this through the reserved **`Kernel\Capabilities`** seam and run the persistent mutation from **`Kernel\Activation::activate()`** (role/cap grants must persist — do not re-run on every `init`).
- **CPT capabilities (closes the 2.1 false-isolation gap):** the nine CPTs currently use `'map_meta_cap' => true` with **no** `capability_type`/`capabilities`, so they inherit bare default `post` caps — a *false impression of isolation* (`deferred-work.md` Story 2.1). Add an explicit mapping so a member cannot manage others' INK content through inherited `post` caps. Keep it **minimal and brownfield-safe**; do not redesign authoring (Epic 5/6).
- **Taxonomy term-management caps (closes the 2.2 fork-vocabulary gap):** the four taxonomies use the default `manage_categories` term caps (`deferred-work.md` Story 2.2). Add a `capabilities` arg so **`manage_terms`/`edit_terms`/`delete_terms` are staff/editor-only** — a member must not be able to fork the controlled vocabulary. (`assign_terms` may stay broad if a flow needs it.)
- **Deny-everyone guard (Epic-2 recurring bug class):** the Capabilities registry currently grants its caps to **nobody** (deliberate stub). When you gate a *live* path on a cap here, make sure that cap is actually granted to the intended role in this story — do not recreate the "stub denies everyone incl. admins" failure that broke 2.1–2.4 writes.
- **What stays deferred to Epic 5:** the full editorial-role policy, per-tier author caps, the promotion-UI authorization scope (`auth_callback` ignores `$object_id` — `deferred-work.md` 2.3). Set only what's needed to close the 2.1/2.2 isolation gaps; **note the rest**, don't build it.

### ⭐ The onboarding-state mechanism (one-time, no re-nag, graceful)

- A single `ink_`-prefixed user-meta flag (suggested `ink_onboarding_complete`) on the **Story-2.3 substrate** (`register_meta('user', …)` with sanitise + default + the shared `is_scalar()` guard). Unset/false ⇒ show onboarding; completing or skipping ⇒ set true. This drives AC-1's one-time behaviour (a returning, already-onboarded/dismissed lid is not re-nagged).
- **Self-set, own-record** (unlike the staff-gated `ink_writer_tier`): the lid sets *their own* onboarding flag; the `auth_callback` / write path authorizes the own-record case only, with nonce + sanitise discipline.
- It stores **nothing else**: no lidmaatskap, no entitlement, no tier re-write, no intent flag. THE conflation rule: `src/Accounts/` carries **zero `Ink\Entitlement` reference**.
