Fair

This is a strong, unusually disciplined brownfield PRD: a real thesis (developmental wedge, "resonance over reach"), a load-bearing conflation rule enforced from glossary through FRs to tests, honest and triaged scope, and counter-metrics that guard the thesis rather than decorate it. All seven rubric dimensions land strong or adequate — no broken dimensions, and the rubric lens surfaced no critical findings.

Two additional reviewers materially shift the picture. Between them they surface six critical build-readiness gaps the quality lens did not weight: (1) the primary success metrics measure activity (visits, feedback presence, participation) rather than the developmental thesis the Vision stakes everything on; (2) the migration's entitlement lifecycle "rides the DB clone" and is verified, not time-tested across the expiry/suspension dimension that is the real post-launch failure mode; (3) reclassifying thousands of legacy posts has no accuracy bar and a skryfwerk holding bucket with no exit policy, while SM-1's "100% survive" passes even if the public library is gutted; and (4) POPIA member-deletion fan-out across the relational graph (follows, reading lists, reactions, ratings, winner records, audit log) is undefined, with a self-contradictory "anonymised/attributed" authorship rule and no orphaned-author mechanism. None of these invalidate the design — but they should be resolved before greenlighting Phase 3, which is why the actionable grade is Fair rather than the near-Excellent the rubric alone would suggest.

Two cross-document defects also need fixing before downstream extraction: a duplicate FR-58 ID with a dangling auto-renew reference, and an addendum that still encodes the abandoned "choose intent" signup step that FR-2/OQ-15 explicitly killed.

Adversarial review

review-adversarial-general.md

Verdict: conditional greenlight. Vocabulary/layering/conflation-rule discipline is genuinely build-ready, but the PRD measures its developmental thesis with activity metrics, hides the riskiest migration work behind unverified "ride the clone" assumptions, and bundles a ~109-story P0+P1 scope onto a solo builder with no launch date. Build the foundation + migration spike first; do not commit the full P0+P1 surface as one scope.

critical

C-1 · Success metrics measure activity, not the stated thesis

§1; §15 SM-4/SM-6/SM-7
The Vision stakes everything on writers getting better; no SM measures it. SM-4 (visits), SM-6 (feedback presence), SM-7 (participation) are all activity proxies — the product can "succeed" while every writer's craft stagnates.
Fix: add ≥1 primary SM that operationalizes craft growth (e.g. % of writers who publish a revised follow-up after receiving voorstel/insig, or a periodic editorial-judged quality sample).
critical

C-2 · Migration's load-bearing requirements are "verify," not "build"; the riskiest data path is untested by design

§10 MR-5; §15 SM-1; addendum E
Subscriptions "ride the DB clone, no import script" and are only "verified." No time-mocked test proves a migrated subscription auto-suspends on its real expiry date weeks after cutover. The thing most likely to silently break (wrongful suspension / revenue leak in week 3) has the weakest verification.
Fix: add a time-mocked entitlement-lifecycle acceptance test (active → near-expiry → expired → suspended → renewed) against migrated records in NFR-9, plus a rollback/reconciliation plan.
critical

C-3 · Reclassification of "several thousand" posts has no success bar; fallback bucket has no exit

§10 MR-6, Risk 2; §3/§4.9 skryfwerk
"Bounded" manual pass is undefined; no misclassification threshold; skryfwerk has no drain policy. SM-1's "100% survive" passes even if 30% of the library lands in an invisible bucket — "survive" and "correctly typed" are conflated.
Fix: promote a measurable classification target to an SM (≤X% in skryfwerk at cutover; named owner + timeline to drain); define "bounded".

§7 NFR-1; §15 SM-2; addendum D

Fix: restate as launch gate (zero on enumerated surfaces) + regression SLA (new leak → remediated within N days); accept the non-zero window.
